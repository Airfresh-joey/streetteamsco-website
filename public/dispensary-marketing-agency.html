Cannabis Compliance: What's Legal in Dispensary Street Marketing

Compliance is the most important consideration in any dispensary marketing program. State cannabis regulators take advertising violations seriously, and a single misstep can result in fines, license issues, or worse. Street Teams Co maintains active compliance protocols across every legal-cannabis state. Here are the standard rules we work within:

Distance Restrictions From Sensitive Locations

Almost every legal-cannabis state restricts cannabis marketing within a defined distance of schools, parks, playgrounds, daycares, libraries, and youth-oriented facilities. Standard distances are 500-1,000 feet, with some states going as far as 2,500 feet. Our compliance team maps every activation route against your state's rules before deployment to ensure no street team or sign spinner positions within a restricted radius.

21+ (or 18+ Medical) Audience Compliance

Cannabis marketing materials cannot be designed to attract underage audiences. This means no cartoon characters, no imagery resembling youth-oriented brands, no targeting of imagery or messaging to minors. All ambassadors deployed by Street Teams Co are themselves 21+ (or 18+ in medical-only states) and are trained to age-gate every interaction. Loyalty signup forms include 21+ attestation. No materials are handed to anyone visibly under age.

No Sampling of Cannabis Products Off-Premises

Cannabis product samples cannot be distributed off-premises in any state. Even on-premises sampling is prohibited in most states. Compliant alternatives include free pre-roll giveaways with first-purchase (where state-legal at the dispensary itself), branded merchandise such as t-shirts and lighters, accessory promotions, discount codes, and loyalty enrollment incentives that drive customers to the licensed retail location for compliant fulfillment.

Required Disclosures and Warnings

Many states require cannabis advertising materials to include specific warnings such as "21+", "Keep out of reach of children", impairment warnings, or pregnancy warnings. Our team reviews every flyer, signup card, and signage piece against the rule book in your operating state and ensures all required disclosures are present and prominent.

Local Right-of-Way and Sign Rules

Sidewalk sign spinners and human directionals are subject to local right-of-way rules in addition to state cannabis advertising rules. Some cities ban sign spinning entirely. Others require business-license attestation or limit hours. Our market-by-market compliance team checks local ordinances before deploying any sidewalk activation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dispensary Marketing

What does a dispensary marketing agency do?

A dispensary marketing agency helps cannabis dispensary owners drive foot traffic and customer acquisition using channels that comply with state cannabis advertising rules. Because Google, Meta, and TikTok restrict cannabis ads, dispensary marketing leans on grand openings, sign spinners, neighborhood flyering, loyalty signups, foot-traffic-driving street teams, in-store events, 4/20 activations, and competitor conquest campaigns.

How much does dispensary marketing cost?

A grand-opening weekend might pair ambassadors with sign spinners, a foot-traffic drive runs daily street teams over 30 days, and multi-location chains coordinate programs per store across all channels. Every program is custom-quoted. Get a free quote.

Is street team marketing legal for cannabis dispensaries?

Yes, in every legal-cannabis state, but every state has specific rules. Common requirements: no marketing within 1,000 feet of schools, parks, or playgrounds; no promotion to anyone under 21 (or 18 medical); no underage-targeting imagery; clear age-restriction language; no off-premises product sampling; required state advertising disclosures. We track rules in every legal state and ensure full compliance.

Can street teams hand out flyers or product samples?

Yes for compliant marketing materials: branded flyers, menus, loyalty cards, discount coupons, and swag. No for cannabis product samples - those cannot be distributed off-premises in any state. Compliant alternatives include free pre-roll giveaways with purchase (in-store), branded merch, and loyalty incentives.

What is a competitor-store conquest campaign?

Street teams positioned within compliant distance of competing dispensaries to capture customers who already shop the category. Tactics include sidewalk activations on public right-of-way near competitor locations, car-side flyering in nearby public parking, and neighborhood canvassing in their catchment area. Most effective when paired with first-time-customer offers and loyalty incentives.
