NIL Compliance: What Brands Need to Know in 2026

NIL compliance is the most important consideration in any college athlete marketing campaign. The rules are still evolving and vary significantly across the 50 states, 32 NCAA Division I conferences, and 350+ Division I universities. Here are the core compliance pillars Street Teams Co builds into every NIL campaign:

State-Level NIL Statutes

Most states with major college athletics have passed NIL legislation. Some states have detailed statutes governing what athletes can and cannot do, others have minimal regulation and rely on NCAA and conference policy. The state of the institution where the athlete is enrolled is what controls. Street Teams Co tracks NIL law in every state and adapts campaign structures, disclosure requirements, and activation tactics accordingly.

NCAA and Conference Policy

The NCAA's NIL guidelines apply across all member institutions. Major conferences (SEC, Big Ten, ACC, Big 12) layer their own additional policies on top. Common rules include: no NIL deals as inducements during recruiting; no NIL payments tied to athletic performance or roster status; required disclosure of NIL deals to athletic department compliance; bans on certain product categories at many schools (alcohol, tobacco, gambling, adult content); and restrictions on use of university trademarks without separate licensing.

Individual University Policies

Each Division I university has its own NIL policy and compliance review process. Athletes must typically disclose deals to compliance, and certain on-campus activations require pre-approval. Some schools have exclusive NIL platform partnerships (Opendorse, INFLCR, Altius Sports Partners). Street Teams Co coordinates directly with compliance offices to pre-clear activations and ensure athletes meet their disclosure requirements.

University Trademark and Likeness Rules

Athletes can use their own NIL but cannot freely use university trademarks, logos, color schemes, mascots, or facilities without separate licensing. This typically requires a licensing agreement with IMG, Learfield, Playfly, or another rights holder depending on the school. Brands wanting both athlete NIL plus university marks structure dual deals: NIL with the athlete and licensing with the rights holder.

Group Licensing and Roster Deals

Some schools and conferences have established group licensing programs that allow brands to access an entire team or roster for use in licensed merchandise and campaigns. Programs vary by school. We coordinate group-licensing opportunities through the appropriate rights holder.

Frequently Asked Questions About College NIL Marketing

What is NIL marketing?

NIL marketing is brand marketing involving Name, Image, and Likeness deals with college athletes. NIL became legal across NCAA athletics in July 2021 and grew to a $1B+ market in 2026. Agencies help brands structure compliant athlete deals, execute campus activations, run game-day stadium experiences, coordinate with NIL collectives, and measure performance. The category sits alongside traditional collegiate players like IMG, Learfield, and Playfly.

How much does a NIL marketing campaign cost?

Athlete deal fees run $500 for a single mid-major social post to $1M+ for top-of-roster Power 5 deals. Activation execution runs $5,000-$50,000 per campus per event day. Multi-campus programs combining 10-30 athlete deals across 5-15 schools typically run $250,000-$2M. We handle execution while your athlete-deal partner or in-house team handles athlete contracts.

Are NIL deals legal in every state?

NIL is legal at the NCAA level nationwide, but state laws and university policies vary. Athletes typically must disclose deals to athletic department compliance, and restrictions apply including no-inducement during recruiting, no university trademark use without licensing, and bans on certain product categories (alcohol, tobacco, gambling, adult content) at many schools. We work with university compliance offices and athlete reps for full compliance.

What is a NIL collective?

NIL collectives are independent organizations formed by alumni, boosters, and supporters of a specific university athletics program to organize NIL deals for that school's athletes. Most Power Five and many Division I programs have associated collectives. Examples include Texas One Fund, 615 Together (Tennessee), and Champions Circle (Michigan). Brands often work with collectives to access groups of athletes at a specific school.

Can you run game-day activations at college stadiums?

Yes. We coordinate stadium-adjacent activations at major venues including The Big House (Michigan), Bryant-Denny (Alabama), Tiger Stadium (LSU), Kyle Field (Texas A&M), Beaver Stadium (Penn State), Neyland Stadium (Tennessee), Sanford Stadium (Georgia), Cameron Indoor (Duke), and 100+ other major college venues. Activations include street teams, fan engagement, branded merchandise, athlete autograph signings, and content capture.

Do you support March Madness and bowl game NIL activations?

Yes. We staff host-city activations around Final Four and Sweet 16 destinations, fan fests at College Football Playoff cities, bowl game activations in Pasadena, New Orleans, Glendale, Atlanta, Tampa, and other host cities. Activations include athlete appearances (where compliant), autograph signings, fan zone staffing, brand merchandise drops, and content capture.

How do you measure NIL campaign performance?

Standard metrics: impressions and engagement on athlete-posted content, traffic from athlete and campus channels, in-store and online sales lift in target campus markets, fan opt-in capture, and content production assets. NIL-specific metrics: athlete-tier benchmarking, conference ROI comparisons (SEC vs Big Ten), and campus-market sales lift versus matched-non-college market controls.
