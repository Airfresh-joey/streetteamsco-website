How We Evaluated These Agencies

We assessed each trade show staffing agency across eight criteria that matter most to exhibitors:

  1. Staff quality and vetting: How rigorously do they screen, interview, and evaluate talent?
  2. Training capabilities: Do they provide brand-specific training, or just show up day-of?
  3. Trade show expertise: Is trade show staffing a core service or a side offering?
  4. Geographic coverage: Can they staff major convention cities reliably?
  5. Pricing transparency: Do they provide clear, itemized pricing?
  6. Backup guarantees: What happens when a staff member cancels?
  7. Reporting and accountability: What data do you get during and after the show?
  8. Client reputation: What do exhibitors say about working with them?

How to Choose the Right Trade Show Staffing Agency

Selecting the right agency starts with understanding your specific needs. Use this decision framework:

Match the Specialty to Your Industry

A healthcare company exhibiting at HIMSS needs an agency that understands compliance requirements. A tech startup at CES needs staff who can demo complex products. A food brand at Natural Products Expo needs sampling experts with food safety training. The best generalist is rarely the best specialist.

Verify Trade Show Experience

Not all event staffing is trade show staffing. Trade shows require staff who can qualify leads quickly, engage B2B professionals who are being approached by dozens of exhibitors, present technical information credibly, and maintain energy and professionalism for 8-10 hour days. Ask for trade show-specific references and case studies. Use our 20-point agency evaluation checklist for a thorough assessment.

Prioritize Training Over Price

An undertrained booth team wastes every dollar you spent on exhibit space, booth design, travel, and logistics. The hourly rate difference between a budget agency and a quality agency is typically $10-$20 per hour. Over a 3-day show, that is $1,000-$2,000 more for dramatically better representation, lead quality, and ROI.

Demand Reporting

Your agency should provide daily lead counts, staff performance data, qualitative feedback on attendee engagement, and a post-show summary with recommendations for improvement. If an agency cannot tell you how many leads your team generated, they are not measuring the things that matter.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does trade show staffing cost?

Booth staff cost $25-$65/hr, demo specialists $35-$80/hr, and narrators $50-$150/hr. A typical 3-day show with 4 staff and 1 team lead costs $5,000-$14,000 all-in. Every campaign is custom-quoted to your goals, team size, and market. Get a free quote.

What should I look for in a trade show staffing agency?

Evaluate trade show-specific experience, staff vetting and training, geographic coverage in convention cities, backup staffing guarantees, transparent pricing, on-site management, and client references from similar shows. Use our 20-point checklist.

How far in advance should I book trade show staff?

Book 4-8 weeks ahead for standard shows and 8-12 weeks for major conventions like CES, SXSW, and NRF. Rush bookings under 2 weeks cost 15-25% more and limit staff selection.

What types of staff can agencies provide for trade shows?

Brand ambassadors, product demo specialists, trade show narrators, registration staff, lead qualification specialists, bilingual staff, technical experts, hospitality staff, and team leads. The best agencies match specific skills to your booth needs.

Should I use a local or national trade show staffing agency?

Use a national agency if you exhibit at 3+ shows per year in different cities. National agencies provide consistency, standardized training, and the ability to scale. Use a local agency only if you exhibit exclusively in one market.
