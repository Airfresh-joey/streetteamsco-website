Hospitality Is Where LA 2028 Brands Win or Lose

During the 2028 Summer Games, the most important brand moments in Los Angeles will not happen on the field — they will happen in the sponsor suites, VIP lounges, hotel hospitality spaces, and corporate activations where brands host their most valuable guests. These are high-stakes environments: executives, clients, partners, and influencers judging your brand by the quality of the experience and the people delivering it.

That is exactly why hospitality staffing is one of the most consequential decisions in any 2028 activation plan. A flawless check-in, a host who remembers a guest's name, a lounge that runs smoothly through a packed session day — these are the details that turn a hospitality investment into relationships and renewals. The wrong staff, by contrast, can undermine a six- or seven-figure program in minutes.

Hospitality & VIP Roles We Staff for LA 2028

A complete hospitality program needs the right person in every role. Here is what we provide:

  • Hosts & hostesses — Warm, polished, on-brand greeters for sponsor suites, lounges, and hospitality entrances who set the tone the moment a guest arrives.
  • Registration & check-in teams — Fast, accurate, friendly credential and guest-list management that keeps lines short and first impressions strong.
  • Lounge & suite attendants — Attentive staff who keep hospitality spaces running smoothly, anticipate guest needs, and maintain a premium environment all day.
  • Brand ambassadors — Trained representatives who engage VIP guests, tell your brand story, and create memorable, personal interactions.
  • Bilingual & multilingual staff — Hosts who can welcome international guests in their own language, essential for the global 2028 audience.
  • Hospitality leads & coordinators — On-site managers who own scheduling, briefings, and a single point of contact across every venue.

Because the 2028 Games draw an enormous international audience, bilingual and multilingual staffing is a hospitality essential, not a nice-to-have. The majority of our available LA hospitality staff are bilingual in English and Spanish, with Mandarin, Korean, French, Japanese, German, and Portuguese speakers available to match your guest list and make every visitor feel genuinely welcomed.

Frequently Asked Questions: LA 2028 Hospitality Staffing

Can you staff sponsor suites and VIP hospitality lounges during the 2028 Games?

Yes. Sponsor suites, VIP lounges, and corporate hospitality spaces are some of the highest-stakes environments during the 2028 Summer Games. We provide polished hosts and hostesses, check-in and registration teams, lounge attendants, brand ambassadors, and hospitality support trained to represent premium brands in front of executives, clients, and high-value guests across LA-area venues and hotels.

Do you provide bilingual hospitality staff for international guests?

Yes. The 2028 Summer Games bring an enormous international audience to Los Angeles, and hospitality programs need staff who can welcome guests in their own language. Absolutely. Many 2028 hospitality programs span hotels, sponsor lounges, and venue suites across Downtown LA, Inglewood, Santa Monica, Anaheim, and beyond. We build coordinated, centrally managed teams with a single point of contact, consistent training and standards across every location, and the staffing depth to scale up for peak days.
