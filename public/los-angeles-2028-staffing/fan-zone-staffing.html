Fan Zones: Where the 2028 Games Crowd Gathers Without a Ticket

For every spectator inside a venue during the 2028 Summer Games, there will be many more gathered at fan zones across Los Angeles. These free public live sites, festival grounds, and big-screen viewing plazas are where the city actually celebrates the Games. They are open to everyone, they run from morning to late evening, and they pull in families, tourists, locals, and lapsed sports fans who would never buy a ticket but absolutely show up for the atmosphere. For a brand, that combination of scale, dwell time, and open access is gold.

What makes fan zones uniquely valuable is the mood of the crowd. People arrive relaxed, social, and primed to try things, take photos, and share moments online. They are not rushing to their seats the way ticketed attendees are, so they will actually stop, talk to your brand ambassadors, accept a sample, and play with an interactive activation. That receptiveness is exactly what turns a busy public space into measurable trial, opt-ins, and content for your channels.

Street Teams Co staffs these high-density gathering spots with crews built specifically for the chaos and energy of festival environments. Our people know how to read a crowd, hold a line, keep engagement counts, and stay on-message through a long, loud event day. Whether you want a single signature presence at one marquee live site or a coordinated footprint across every major viewing area in the metro, we put the right team on the ground. Event staffing built for this moment starts with the right people.

How Fan Zone Staffing Works for the 2028 Games

A great fan zone activation is part logistics and part showmanship. Here is how we approach the opportunities these public gathering spots create:

  • Big-screen viewing plazas — Wherever the city sets up live screens, captive crowds linger for hours between sessions. Sampling crews and brand ambassadors work the dwell time with trial, demos, and giveaways.
  • Festival-style live sites — Food, music, and entertainment grounds turn the Games into an all-day outing. Costumed teams, photo activations, and interactive booths thrive in this party atmosphere.
  • Public plazas and open-air gathering points — Pedestrian squares and outdoor commons fill organically. High-energy street teams capture the overflow and direct foot traffic toward your activation footprint.
  • Crowd-engagement and hosting roles — Trained hosts emcee games, run prize wheels, and keep energy high so your brand owns the most memorable corner of the fan zone.
  • Content capture and amplification — Our teams generate shareable moments and capture user-generated content, extending a physical fan zone presence into far greater digital reach.
  • Multi-site coordination — One account manager, local leads at every viewing area, and consistent execution so your brand shows up the same way across every fan zone in LA.

Bilingual staffing is essential in this setting. Fan zones draw the most international crowd of the entire 2028 Games, since you do not need a ticket to attend. The majority of our available LA fan zone staff are fully bilingual in English and Spanish, with Korean, Mandarin, Armenian, Tagalog, and additional languages available so your brand connects authentically with every visitor in the crowd.

Frequently Asked Questions: Fan Zone Staffing

What is a fan zone and why does it matter for 2028 Games marketing?

A fan zone is a free, high-density public gathering space — think live sites with big screens, festival grounds, and outdoor plazas — where crowds gather to watch and celebrate the 2028 Summer Games without holding a ticket. Because these areas are open to everyone and draw enormous all-day foot traffic, they are some of the most valuable activation environments in Los Angeles for brands that want reach without venue sponsorship rights.

What kind of staff do you provide for fan zones and festivals?

We staff fan zones with brand ambassadors, sampling crews, crowd-engagement hosts, costumed characters, photo-activation attendants, and field supervisors. Teams are trained to work loud, busy, high-energy crowds and to keep engagement counts, capture content, and manage inventory across long event days. Bilingual English and Spanish staff are standard, with additional languages available.

Can you cover multiple fan zones across Los Angeles at the same time?

Yes. The 2028 Summer Games will activate viewing sites across the entire LA metro at once. We build coordinated teams that cover several fan zones simultaneously under one account manager, with local leads at each site so your brand shows up everywhere with consistent execution and a single point of contact.

How much does fan zone staffing cost for the 2028 Games?

Every fan zone program is custom-quoted based on team size, number of sites, hours, roles, and language needs. There are no published rate cards because no two activations are alike. Tell us your brief and we will return a detailed staffing plan and a free quote.
