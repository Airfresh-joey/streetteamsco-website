Pasadena: The Iconic Venue District of the 2028 Games

Few venues in the world carry the prestige of the Rose Bowl, and during the 2028 Summer Games this legendary Pasadena stadium brings massive, celebratory crowds to the San Gabriel Valley. Set in the Arroyo Seco against the San Gabriel Mountains, the Rose Bowl is one of the most recognizable backdrops in sports, and it draws spectators who treat a session there as a full-day occasion.

Pasadena's advantage is the pairing of that iconic venue with a genuinely walkable, upscale companion district just minutes away. Old Town Pasadena and the Colorado Boulevard corridor offer block after block of shopping, dining, and nightlife that naturally absorbs the pre- and post-session crowd. Where the Rose Bowl itself sits in a parklike setting with controlled access, Old Town provides the dense, street-level foot traffic that brand activations thrive on.

Street Teams Co staffs Pasadena and the San Gabriel Valley regularly and understands the flow between the Arroyo, the shuttle and parking systems, and the Old Town core. Our brand ambassadors and street teams know how to bridge the gap between the venue and the city, capturing fans at the Rose Bowl approaches and again as they spill into the bars and restaurants of Colorado Boulevard.

Activation Opportunities in Pasadena

Pasadena pairs an iconic stadium with a dense, walkable companion district. Here is where brands win:

  • Rose Bowl approaches & Arroyo — The parklike setting funnels celebratory, all-day crowds toward defined entry routes, ideal for brand ambassador intercepts and sampling.
  • Shuttle & parking systems — With managed access to the Arroyo, the shuttle pickup and parking zones become captive-audience choke points for engagement.
  • Old Town Pasadena — The walkable shopping and dining core absorbs the pre- and post-session crowd, perfect for street teams and experiential activations.
  • Colorado Boulevard corridor — The lively nightlife strip delivers strong evening foot traffic for guerrilla marketing and beverage sampling.
  • Old Pasadena plazas & courtyards — The district's open courtyards create natural dwell zones for branded installations and photo activations.
  • Metro Gold/A Line stations — Transit arrivals into Pasadena concentrate foot traffic at station exits, ideal for intercept-style ambassador deployments.

Pasadena and the San Gabriel Valley are home to large, established Latino and Asian American communities, and the global crowd arriving for the 2028 Games adds further reach. The majority of our available Pasadena staff are fully bilingual in English and Spanish, with Mandarin, Cantonese, and additional languages available to reflect the San Gabriel Valley's communities and connect with every visitor.

Yes. The Rose Bowl draws massive, celebratory, all-day crowds to the Arroyo Seco. We deploy brand ambassadors, sampling crews, and street teams at the venue approaches, shuttle and parking systems, and the nearby Old Town Pasadena district before, during, and after sessions.

Should I activate at the Rose Bowl or in Old Town Pasadena?

Both, ideally.
