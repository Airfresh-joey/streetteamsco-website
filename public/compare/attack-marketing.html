Detailed Analysis: Pricing and Transparency

Attack Marketing uses custom project-based pricing that reflects the creative complexity of their campaigns. Wild posting campaigns, guerrilla stunts, and experiential activations each carry different cost structures depending on market, scale, materials, permits, and creative production requirements. This custom approach is standard for creative-led agencies but makes upfront budget planning more difficult. Brands should expect to go through a proposal process before understanding total campaign costs.

Street Teams Co publishes rate ranges on their pricing page: street team members at $25-$50/hr, brand ambassadors at $25-$45/hr, event staff at $25-$55/hr, and promotional models at $35-$65/hr. Every proposal itemizes each cost component. For a street team campaign, the proposal clearly shows how many staff, for how many hours, in which cities, plus management fees, materials, and any additional costs. This transparency enables fast budget approvals, accurate cost forecasting, and easy comparison against other vendors. For brands running regular street marketing programs, the predictability of published rates simplifies ongoing planning and vendor management.

Detailed Analysis: Technology and Accountability

Campaign accountability is a persistent challenge in street marketing. How does a brand manager in Dallas know that their street team in Portland actually showed up, worked their full shift, and engaged the target number of consumers? Attack Marketing provides campaign reporting with photos and metrics, which is standard for the industry. However, this reporting is typically compiled after the campaign or at designated checkpoints.

Street Teams Co addresses the accountability gap with technology built specifically for field marketing operations. Every street team member is tracked via GPS in real time. Clients can open their dashboard and see exactly where their team is at any moment during active campaigns. Timestamped, geotagged photos are uploaded throughout the shift, verifying location and activity. Consumer engagement counts and material distribution numbers update live. If a team member is late, out of position, or underperforming, the client and the account manager know immediately and can intervene. The Show-Up Guarantee adds a contractual layer: if a confirmed team member does not arrive, Street Teams Co provides a replacement or credits the client. This combination of technology and guarantee fundamentally changes the accountability equation in street marketing.
