The Research: Why Sampling Converts

Independent research consistently shows that letting consumers physically try a product drives trial and purchase far more effectively than advertising alone. Selected, sourced findings:

~35% of consumers who try a sample go on to purchase
Source: sampling industry research, via Eventeem
73% are induced to try a new product when given a free sample (vs. far fewer from a TV ad)
Source: sampling effectiveness research, via Eventeem
Physical product sampling can lift sales by ~40%+ vs. no sampling (one study: 41.6%)
Source: industry research, via Shipfusion
Impulse categories convert at 35–75%; considered purchases 10–15%
Source: sampling conversion research, via Peekage

Figures above are third-party industry research, attributed to their sources. Street Teams Co's "By the Numbers" data is first-party.

How to Measure Sampling ROI

The brands that win at sampling instrument it. Track samples distributed, trial-to-purchase conversion via unique QR codes or coupon redemption, cost per sample, cost per acquisition, and sell-through lift versus a control market using retail scan data (SPINS, IRI/Circana, Nielsen).
