Beverage & Alcohol Brands Own the World Cup

No category activates harder around the World Cup than beverage and alcohol. Beer, spirits, hard seltzer, soda, energy, water, and non-alcoholic brands all compete for the same thirsty, celebratory, summer match-day crowds — in fan zones, bars, beer gardens, watch parties, retail, and on-premise accounts. For these brands, the World Cup is one of the biggest trial-and-trade windows of the year, and the people pouring, sampling, and selling are the front line of the whole investment.

That makes activation staffing a make-or-break decision. A fast, friendly, compliant sampling team keeps lines moving and product cold; a polished bar-takeover crew turns a sponsored account into a branded experience; a sharp brand ambassador turns a free pour into a follower and a future purchase. The wrong team — slow, off-brand, or careless with compliance — can stall a six-figure program in front of thousands of fans.

Street Teams Co staffs beverage and alcohol activations across all 11 US host cities — Miami, New York/New Jersey, Los Angeles, Dallas, and the rest — with vetted, bilingual, age-verification-trained staff who execute fast and on-brand from kickoff to the final whistle.

Compliant Alcohol Activation, Done Right

Alcohol activations carry requirements that beverage sampling alone does not. Our staff execute them with discipline:

  • Age & ID verification — Staff experienced in checking IDs and verifying age before any pour or sample, the non-negotiable foundation of any alcohol program.
  • Responsible service practices — Trained to recognize and decline over-service, manage limits, and keep activations safe and on-brand.
  • Licensed & certified staff where required — Where a market or venue requires specific server certifications or licensed pourers, we source staff who meet those requirements.
  • Documentation & compliance coordination — We coordinate with your compliance team and venue on the paperwork, permits, and standards alcohol programs require.
  • Cold-chain & restock logistics — Coolers, ice, back-of-house restocking, and timed distribution so product stays cold and lines stay fast through peak match windows.
  • On-brand presentation — Uniformed, briefed, and rehearsed so every pour reflects your brand standards.

Because the World Cup draws an enormous international audience, bilingual and multilingual staffing matters here too — the majority of our available staff are bilingual in English and Spanish, with additional languages available to connect with fans from every competing nation.

Frequently Asked Questions: World Cup Beverage Activation

Can you staff beverage and alcohol brand activations during the World Cup 2026?

Yes. Beverage and alcohol brands are among the largest experiential spenders during the World Cup, and the tournament's match-day crowds are a prime audience. We staff product sampling teams, brand ambassadors, beer garden and bar-takeover staff, and tasting-table teams for watch parties, fan zones, bars, and on-premise activations across all 11 US host cities.

Is your staff trained for responsible alcohol sampling and age verification?

Yes. Alcohol activations require disciplined, compliant execution. Our staff are experienced with ID and age verification, responsible-service practices, and the documentation alcohol programs require. Where a market or venue requires specific certifications or licensed servers, we source staff who meet those requirements and coordinate with your compliance team.

Yes. Cold beverages in summer crowds are a logistics challenge as much as a staffing one.
